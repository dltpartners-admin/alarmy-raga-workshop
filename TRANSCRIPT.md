## [Slide 3] The Real Problem - 2 min

> So let's talk about the elephant in the room. You all know this number. 10 to 15 percent of your users pay. The rest? You're spending UA money to acquire them, they're using your product every day, and they're generating zero revenue.
>
> You can optimize your paywall all you want. You can A/B test the button color, the price point, the trial length. Maybe you squeeze out another percentage point. Congrats, now 16% pay instead of 15%. **What about the other 84%?**
>
> For Alarmy, this was even worse. Nobody wakes up and thinks "I should pay for my alarm." It's like paying for air. The category WTP is basically zero.
>
> So we had millions of people using our app every single morning, and we were making nothing from most of them. And I bet a lot of you are sitting in a similar situation right now - different category, same math.
>
> **The question isn't how to convert more. It's what to do with the ones who will never convert.**

---

## [Slide 4] The Paywall Decision - 2 min

> This is where we made the call that changed everything.
>
> Hard paywall: you force the decision. Pay or leave. Your conversion rate goes up, sure. But every user who doesn't pay - gone. Forever. DAU bleeds out. In a low-WTP category? That's suicide. You're burning 90% of your users to squeeze 10%.
>
> We went soft. Lower conversion, yes. But those free users? They stay. They build the habit. They wake up with Alarmy tomorrow, and the day after, and the day after that. DAU compounds.
>
> And here's the thing you already know but maybe haven't internalized: **subscription conversion is basically a Day 1 event.** After that first session, almost nobody converts. Ever. So within 24 hours, you know who's paying and who isn't. That's it.
>
> Result: 10% subscribers. 90% free. Every single day.
>
> Our team had a real debate about this. Some people said "that 90% are failed conversions." I said they're an untapped asset.
>
> **We chose to keep users alive. Then we figured out how to monetize all of them.**

---

## [Slide 5] Different Monetization DNA - 2 min

> OK so here's the insight that unlocked everything. And it's kind of obvious once you see it, but most companies miss it.
>
> Your users aren't one group. They're three completely different species.
>
> **10% are subscribers.** They decided on Day 1. High WTP, want ad-free, done. Monetize via subscription. Don't touch them.
>
> **5% are "maybe" users.** On the fence. You have about 3 days to convert them with a well-timed discount. After that, they drift to free-forever. Small window, act fast or don't bother.
>
> **85% will never pay.** Never. Not in a million years. But they use Alarmy every single morning. They're your most habitual users. And they are your ad revenue engine.
>
> Now - the question everyone asks: "Doesn't adding ads cannibalize subscriptions?"
>
> No. And I'll tell you why. Subscribers never see ads. Free users were never going to subscribe. **These populations don't overlap.** We've measured this to death. Cannibalization is a myth. It's a boogeyman that's costing you money every day you don't address it.
>
> Subscriptions first. Ads second. **Almost zero overlap.**

## [Slide 6a] The UX Fear - 1.5 min

> Alright. I can feel the room. "But Goodway, if I put ads in my app, users will leave."
>
> I hear this every single week. And I get it. Your designer will hate you. Your PM will panic. Your founder will say "we're not that kind of app."
>
> But let me push back on something. There's this idea that "UX-friendly ads" means invisible ads. Tiny banners nobody sees. Rewarded videos buried three screens deep. You know what invisible ads are? **Bad ads with low CPM.** You're sacrificing revenue for comfort that users don't even register.
>
> UX-friendly doesn't mean invisible. It means **integrated.** The ad is clearly an ad. But it fits the moment. It fits the flow. Users accept it because the app just delivered value.
>
> Alarmy wakes you up. That's the core value. The moment you dismiss the alarm - you're awake, you're alert, mission accomplished. That's when the ad appears. Peak attention. Peak receptivity. Not random. Not interruptive. **Integrated.**
>
> And please - stop copying what gaming apps do. **10 native ads do not equal 1 rewarded video.** Your app has its own DNA. Find the format that fits it.

## [Slide 6b] The 5-Year Journey - 2 min

> Now - finding that answer? It took us five years. I want to be honest about that.
>
> **2020.** We went big. Interstitial right in the core journey. Right after the alarm. Revenue doubled overnight. 2x. Retention metrics? Totally fine. D1 through D7, no movement.
>
> And we pulled it. We killed 2x revenue.
>
> Why? The data said yes. The users said no. The feedback was brutal - "you're ruining the one thing this app does well." And we had a real fight internally about it. Data vs. humans. We chose humans.
>
> Was that the right call? Honestly, I still wonder sometimes. But it forced us to find a better answer.
>
> **2022.** Rewarded Video in ringtones. Safe. Outside the core. Limited upside. But we learned how our users react to ads.
>
> **2024.** Interstitial in snooze. Still outside the core. Incremental revenue. More data.
>
> **2025.** Light Popup in alarm dismiss. Back in the core journey - but a completely different format. Fullscreen but instantly closable. Natural layout. Doesn't break the flow.
>
> **Zero retention drop. +30% revenue. CTR 2.3%, eCPM $2.7.** Today it's 15% of our total ad revenue.
>
> We chose UX over 2x revenue in 2020. Five years later, we got both.

## [Slide 7] The Math - 2 min

> Let me hit you with some math. Because the scale dynamics here are wild.
>
> Alarmy shows about **150 impressions per user per day.** I know - that sounds insane. But Alarmy doesn't feel ad-heavy. Internal pacing handles that. The point is: when you have the inventory, even tiny ARPU improvements compound.
>
> Watch this. **$0.007 ARPU lift.** Less than a cent. Times **4 million DAU.** That's **$840K a month.** From less than a cent per user.
>
> A 15% ARPU improvement is a 15% revenue improvement. No new users. No new UA spend. Just better monetization of the users you already have.
>
> But - and I want to be real about this - DAU alone is misleading.
>
> **DAV is not DAU.** Not every user reaches an ad placement every session. Depth matters.
>
> **Geography crushes you.** 100K US DAU is worth roughly 1 million India DAU in ad revenue. If your growth is all emerging markets, your eCPM will humble you.
>
> **Frequency compounds.** A user who opens the app 5 times a day is worth 5x a once-a-day user. Obvious? Sure. But how many of you are actually measuring this?
>
> **DAU is the foundation. But depth of engagement is where the money is.**

## [Slide 8] The Portfolio Effect - 1.5 min

> Here's what happens when you get both engines running.
>
> Subscriptions: conversion-driven. Spiky. Promo-dependent. Great margin, but you're always on the treadmill. One bad month of conversion and your revenue dips.
>
> Ads: DAU-driven. Stable. Scales with engagement. **Near-zero marginal cost.** Every impression is almost pure profit.
>
> Put them together and you get something beautiful: **when one dips, the other holds.** Subscription revenue down this month because you didn't run a promo? Ads are steady. Ad eCPM down because of market conditions? Subscription revenue doesn't care.
>
> **$2.86M monthly. $2.64M operating profit. 93% margin.**
>
> This isn't diversification. **It's risk management.**

## [Slide 9] The Playbook - 2.5 min

> OK so if you want to do this, here's how we actually did it. Not theory. The actual sequence.
>
> **Phase 1: Start at the edges.** Don't go into the core journey. Don't even think about it yet. Put a rewarded video in a secondary feature. Put a banner in a settings-adjacent screen. Low risk. What you're really doing is learning how your specific users react to ads. Every app is different. Gather data before you get ambitious.
>
> **Phase 2: Measure with LTV, not eCPM.** This is where most teams go wrong. eCPM goes up and down with the market - you can't control it. What you can control is Ad LTV. That's lifetime times ARPDAU. **If retention drops 3 points but ARPDAU goes up 10%, does LTV go up?** Yes? Keep the placement. No? Pull it. That's your entire decision framework. Stop staring at eCPM dashboards. They'll just give you anxiety.
>
> **Phase 3: Earn your way back into the core journey.** Once you've built confidence at the edges and you trust your measurement framework - then you go for the core. But with the right format. Our 2020 failure wasn't "ads in core = bad." It was "wrong format in core = bad." The Light Popup is the right format. Took us 5 years to find it.
>
> **5+ years. No shortcuts. But there is a sequence.**

## [Slide 10] The 100% Playbook - 1 min

> Five rules. These are non-negotiable for us.
>
> **One. Low WTP doesn't mean low revenue.** It means you haven't found the right playbook yet.
>
> **Two. Subs and ads users are different people.** Stop worrying about cannibalization. It's not real.
>
> **Three. UX-friendly ads aren't invisible.** Invisible ads are just bad ads. Integrate, don't hide.
>
> **Four. Retention beats eCPM.** Every time. If your ad strategy hurts retention, it's not a strategy. It's a tax.
>
> **Five. Start outside, earn your way in.** Measure with LTV. Be patient. The core journey is earned, not given.
